STEM AI-Resistant Assignment Design

Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Follow the journey from a vulnerable prompt to a secure, authentic assessment in Science & Engineering.

The "Research Report"

Status: Highly Vulnerable
ENGR 101: Introduction to Engineering Spring 2025
Midterm Paper: Renewable Energy

Instructions: Research and write a 5-page report on renewable energy technologies (e.g., solar, wind, hydro).

Summarize the current state of the technology, efficiency rates, and implementation challenges.

  1. Research renewable energy.
  2. Write a standard report.
  3. Summarize existing technologies.
Vulnerability Report
  • Universal Training Data: Renewable energy is a massive topic in LLM training data. AI can generate detailed summaries instantly.
  • Text-Only Output: Writing about engineering is not the same as doing engineering. This format allows AI to bypass actual problem-solving.
  • Passive Consumption: Summarizing existing tech requires no novel thought, design work, or experimentation.

The Engineering Design Shield

In STEM, we defend against AI by requiring students to engage in the iterative, messy process of physical creation and data analysis.

1. Physical Creation

Build something real. AI can describe a wind turbine; it cannot glue the balsa wood blades together or balance the rotor.

2. Iterative Process

Document failures. Require a "Lab Notebook" showing Version 1, why it failed, and how Version 2 improved. AI generates the final answer, not the journey.

3. Data Analysis

Analyze your specific test data. "Why did my solar tracker fail at 2pm?" requires interpreting unique, noisy data.

4. Defense

Peer Q&A. Defending methodology in real-time reveals who truly understands the engineering principles vs. who just memorized text.

The "Engineering Prototype"

Status: AI Resistant
ENGR 101: Introduction to Engineering Spring 2025
Project: Renewable Energy Prototype

Instructions: Design, build, and test a working model of a renewable energy device (e.g., wind turbine, solar cooker).

  • Build: Construct a physical prototype using provided materials.
  • Document: Maintain a lab notebook documenting at least 3 iterations and failure points.
  • Test: Collect efficiency data (voltage/temp) and present findings with peer Q&A defending your methodology.
  1. Design and build a physical prototype.
  2. Document iterations and failures in a notebook.
  3. Analyze specific test data & defend methodology.
Verification Successful

This assignment successfully applies the Engineering Design Shield strategies:

  • Physical Creation: AI cannot build a physical model.
  • Iterative Process: Documenting failure and revision proves human effort and learning.
  • Defense: Real-time Q&A tests deep understanding and prevents rote memorization.
