Local Water Quality Analysis

Target: Environmental Science / Chemistry
Instructions: Investigate the water quality of a local body of water using EPA standards.
  1. Select a specific sampling site (GPS coordinates required). Strategy 1: Local Context
  2. Document site conditions with timestamped photos and field notes. Strategy 2: Personal Experience
  3. Conduct pH and nitrate tests; submit raw Lab Notebook pages. Strategy 3: Process Documentation
  4. Draft the "Results Analysis" section during the 50-minute lab period. Strategy 4: In-Class Work
  5. Present and defend an "outlier data point" to the lab group. Strategy 5: Oral/Multi-Modal
Audit Complete: Maximum Resistance This assignment rates Maximum. It employs all five core strategies: Local Context, Personal Experience, Process Documentation, In-Class Writing, and Oral Defense.

The Five Core Strategies

Physical sciences offer unique opportunities to bypass AI. By combining these five strategies, we create assignments that require human presence, physical interaction, and real-time synthesis.

1. Specific Context

Tie the work to a specific time and place. AI has general knowledge, not knowledge of this playground's friction or this creek's flow rate today.

2. Personal Experience

Require evidence of "being there." Field notes, selfies at the site, or reflections on equipment failures validate the human element.

3. Process Focus

Value the Lab Notebook as much as the Lab Report. The messy raw data, scribbles, and corrections are proof of learning.

4. In-Class Work

Flip the classroom. Have students do the "writing" or "calculating" in the lab under supervision, where you can assist and verify.

5. Multi-Modal

Change the output. Instead of an essay, ask for a video walkthrough of the experiment, a podcast explaining the physics, or an oral defense.
