Business AI-Resistant Assignment Design

Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Follow the journey from a vulnerable prompt to a secure, authentic assessment.

The "Company Report"

Status: Highly Vulnerable
MKT 301: Principles of Marketing Spring 2025
Midterm Project: Corporate Strategy Analysis

Instructions: Select a Fortune 500 company (e.g., Apple, Tesla, Nike) and write a 6-page strategic analysis.

Your report must include a SWOT analysis, a review of their current marketing mix (4 Ps), and recommendations for future growth based on recent financial reports.

  1. Analyze a Fortune 500 company.
  2. Conduct a SWOT Analysis.
  3. Write a 6-page strategic report.
Vulnerability Report
  • Public Data Saturation: Companies like Apple and Tesla are the most written-about entities in the AI's training set. It has millions of SWOT analyses already indexed.
  • Generic Analysis: Without a specific, proprietary data set, the AI can generate a "correct" but generic analysis that requires no actual research.
  • Zero Human Contact: The student can complete this entire assignment without ever speaking to a customer, employee, or stakeholder.

The Business Shield

In Business education, we defend against AI by moving from "passive analysis" of global giants to "active consulting" for local partners.

1. Authentic Partner

Replace Fortune 500s with local small businesses. AI does not know the specific financial struggles of the bakery down the street.

2. Human Friction

Require interaction. Interviews with owners, surveys of real customers, and mystery shopping create data that cannot be synthesized.

3. Applied Strategy

Don't ask "What is a SWOT?" Ask "How does the 'Threat of New Entrants' apply to this specific coffee shop's location?"

4. Singular Context

Every local market is unique. A student analyzing this gym in this zip code creates a dataset of one.

The "Consulting Project"

Status: AI Resistant
MKT 301: Principles of Marketing Spring 2025
Project: Small Business Market Analysis

Instructions: Partner with a local small business to conduct a primary market research analysis.

  • Owner Interview: Conduct a 30-minute interview with the owner regarding their specific business goals and challenges.
  • Primary Data: Design and execute a survey of 25+ potential customers in the local area.
  • Competitor Audit: Physically visit and analyze 3 local competitors.
  1. Partner with a local small business (Real-world context).
  2. Interview owner & survey 25 customers (Primary Data).
  3. Physically visit 3 local competitors (Active Research).
Verification Successful

This assignment successfully applies the Business Shield strategies:

  • Authentic Partner: AI cannot access the internal challenges or financial goals of a specific local small business.
  • Human Friction: The requirement for interviews and surveys forces the student to generate original data rather than retrieving it.
  • Singular Context: The analysis is tied to a specific geographic location (zip code) and real-time competitor landscape.
