Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Analyze a model business assignment and build your own using the Process vs Product approach.

Local Small Business Market Analysis

Target: Business/Marketing Students
Instructions: In this module, you will conduct primary market research for a local small business.
  1. Select a local small business willing to collaborate on this project. Reason: Real-World Relationships
  2. Interview the owner about their specific business challenges and goals. Reason: Primary Research Process
  3. Survey 25+ potential customers in the target market. Reason: Generating New Data
  4. Analyze competitor offerings and present findings to the business owner. Reason: Contextual Application
Audit Complete: High Resistance This assignment rates High. It values the Process (interviewing, surveying, consulting) over just a final written Product.

The Core Shift

Generative AI is a Product Engine—it creates essays, code, and summaries instantly. To resist AI, we must become Process Assessors. We grade the journey, the friction, and the evolution of ideas, not just the final file.

Scaffolding

Break the "Product" (Final Paper) into visible "Processes" (Topic Proposal, Annotated Bibliography, Outline). AI is great at the finish line, but bad at showing the steps to get there.

Original Data

AI relies on existing internet data. Force students to generate new data through interviews, surveys, or experiments. The value is in the collection process.

Friction

Learning involves friction—confusion, dead ends, and revision. AI is frictionless. Ask students to document their "stuck points" and how they overcame them.

Metacognition

Ask "How did you write this?" instead of just "Write this." Reflections, version histories, and oral defenses prove the student did the thinking.
