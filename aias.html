Practical Assessment Design

The AIAS Framework

Four interconnected pillars for creating assignments that resist AI completion while enhancing authentic learning.

Why use AIAS?

Traditional "write an essay" assignments are vulnerable. The most effective AI-resistant assignments incorporate multiple pillars shown above. Even adding one significantly increases assignment integrity.

AI-Resistance Assessment Scale (AIAS)

A 5-level spectrum of AI permission and integration in assessment design.

01

No AI

Controlled Environment

In-person exams, handwritten work, closed-book tests. No AI access permitted.

Use when: Assessing foundational knowledge without technology.
02

AI-Assisted

Limited Use

AI for brainstorming, grammar checking, research starting points. Attribution required.

Use when: Teaching responsible AI use and citation practices.
03

AI-Integrated

Process Focus

AI as collaboration tool. Students must explain decision-making and submit artifacts.

Use when: Emphasizing critical evaluation of AI outputs.
04

AI-Collaborative

Partner Level

Students curate, critique, and improve AI-generated content. Focus on human synthesis.

Use when: Developing AI literacy and critical thinking skills.
05

AI-Enhanced

Full Integration

Meta-cognitive reflection on AI's role. Students explore capabilities and limitations.

Use when: Teaching about AI itself or advanced digital literacy.
