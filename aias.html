Practical Assessment Design

The AIAS Framework

Four interconnected pillars for creating assignments that resist AI completion while enhancing authentic learning.

Why use AIAS?

Traditional "write an essay" assignments are vulnerable. The most effective AI-resistant assignments incorporate multiple pillars below. Even adding one significantly increases assignment integrity.

AI-Resistance Assessment Scale (AIAS)

A 5-level spectrum of AI permission and integration in assessment design.

Level 1

No AI

Controlled environment, no AI access permitted
  • In-person exams, handwritten work, closed-book tests
  • Use when: Assessing foundational knowledge without technology
Level 2

AI-Assisted

Limited AI use with attribution required
  • AI for brainstorming, grammar checking, research starting points
  • Students must document all AI use and cite appropriately
  • Use when: Teaching responsible AI use and citation practices
Level 3

AI-Integrated

AI incorporated with process documentation
  • AI as collaboration tool; students explain decision-making
  • Process artifacts required (prompts, revisions, reflections)
  • Use when: Emphasizing critical evaluation of AI outputs
Level 4

AI-Collaborative

AI as partner with human judgment central
  • Students curate, critique, and improve AI-generated content
  • Focus on evaluation, synthesis, and originality of thought
  • Use when: Developing AI literacy and critical thinking skills
Level 5

AI-Enhanced

Full AI integration with explicit pedagogical purpose
  • Students explore AI capabilities and limitations
  • Meta-cognitive reflection on AI's role in learning
  • Use when: Teaching about AI itself or advanced digital literacy

Quick Decision Tree

Can students complete without AI? Level 1
Need basic AI tools? Level 2
AI as drafting partner? Level 3
AI outputs need curation? Level 4
Teaching about AI? Level 5
