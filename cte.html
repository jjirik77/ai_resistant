Automotive Fault Diagnosis

Target: Automotive Technology / CTE
Instructions: Diagnose and repair a "Customer Vehicle" (lab car) with a specific, physical engine fault.
  1. Perform manual inspection and OBD-II scan on the physical lab vehicle. Strategy: Physical Interaction
  2. Record a 3-minute 'Tech Video' narrating the root cause discovery process. Strategy: Visual Evidence
  3. Trace the electrical fault using a multimeter and circuit diagram. Strategy: Diagnostic Reasoning
  4. Explain the repair and cost estimate to the 'customer' (instructor) in a roleplay. Strategy: Client Simulation
Audit Complete: Maximum Resistance This assignment rates Maximum. CTE offers the ultimate defense: the requirement for physical skill demonstration, visual logging, and oral communication.

The Performance Standard

In Career & Technical Education, we don't just ask "What do you know?" We ask "What can you do?" This performance-based approach is inherently AI-resistant.

1. Physicality

The ultimate firewall. AI generates text; it cannot weld metal, bake a souffl√©, or draw blood. Anchor assessment in physical output.

2. Visual Evidence

Move beyond written reports. Require "Tech Videos," photo logs, or time-lapses of the creation process to prove authorship.

3. Diagnostic Logic

AI is good at general theory, but poor at troubleshooting specific, random physical faults (e.g., "Why is this specific sauce breaking?").

4. Client Simulation

Technical skills mean little if you can't communicate them. Oral defense and roleplay scenarios test real-time understanding.
