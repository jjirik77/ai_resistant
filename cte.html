Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Follow the journey from a vulnerable prompt to a secure, authentic assessment in Career & Technical Education.

The "Written Process"

Status: Highly Vulnerable
AUTO 102: Engine Performance Spring 2025
Written Report: Diagnostic Procedure

Instructions: Write a 3-page report describing the step-by-step procedure for diagnosing a check engine light with Code P0300 (Random Misfire).

Explain the potential causes (fuel, spark, compression) and describe how you would test each system using a multimeter and scan tool.

  1. Describe diagnostic steps.
  2. Explain potential causes.
  3. Write a standard procedure report.
Vulnerability Report
  • Manual-Based Data: Repair manuals are fully digitized online. AI can recite the textbook procedure perfectly without the student ever touching a tool.
  • Text-Only Output: Writing about a physical task is not the same as doing it. AI can fake the knowledge without the skill.
  • Generic Troubleshooting: AI provides the "book answer," not the nuanced, sensory reality of a specific engine's quirks or rust issues.

The Performance Standard

In CTE, we don't just ask "What do you know?" We ask "What can you do?" This performance-based approach is inherently AI-resistant.

1. Physicality

The ultimate firewall. AI generates text; it cannot weld metal, bake a soufflé, or draw blood. Anchor assessment in physical output.

2. Visual Evidence

Move beyond written reports. Require "Tech Videos," photo logs, or time-lapses of the creation process to prove authorship.

3. Diagnostic Logic

AI is good at general theory, but poor at troubleshooting specific, random physical faults (e.g., "Why is this specific wire corroded?").

4. Client Simulation

Technical skills mean little if you can't communicate them. Oral defense and roleplay scenarios test real-time understanding.

The "Live Diagnosis"

Status: AI Resistant
AUTO 102: Engine Performance Spring 2025
Project: Customer Fault Diagnosis (Live Lab)

Instructions: You are assigned a "Customer Vehicle" (Lab Car #4) with a live check engine light. Diagnose the root cause using shop tools.

  • Verification: Verify the concern and pull diagnostic codes (DTCs).
  • Tech Video: Record a 3-minute video narrating your diagnostic path, showing your multimeter/scan tool readings in real-time.
  • Customer Roleplay: Verbalize your findings, the required repair, and the cost estimate to the Service Writer (Instructor).
  1. Diagnose physical bug on lab vehicle.
  2. Record "Tech Video" of diagnostic steps.
  3. Roleplay explanation to customer.
Verification Successful

This assignment successfully applies the Performance Standard strategies:

  • Physicality: AI cannot interact with the physical vehicle or hold the tools needed to diagnose the live bug.
  • Visual Evidence: The "Tech Video" explicitly proves the student performed the work and understands the readings.
  • Client Simulation: Real-time roleplay prevents the student from relying on a pre-generated script.
