Five Proven Strategies
for AI-Resistant Design

Research-based methods to create assignments that resist AI completion while deepening authentic learning.

Highly Specific & Personalized

Make assignments unique to each student via constraints and personal data.
  • Include student-specific data or prior work
  • Individualized constraints or parameters
  • Personalized feedback integration
  • Unique combinations of variables
Example
"Based on your Week 3 reflection where you discussed [specific experience], analyze how [specific concept] applies to your workplace/clinical setting."

Multilayer / Scaffolded

Break assignments into multiple checkpoints to show the thinking process.
  • Progressive drafts with instructor feedback
  • Iterative revision requirements
  • Process documentation (decision logs)
  • Reflections on changes made between drafts
Example
Proposal → Feedback → Revised approach → Data collection → Analysis → Presentation → Final reflection

Local & Community Context

Root assignments in accessible, real-world, non-digital contexts.
  • Local businesses or community partners
  • Primary source interviews
  • Site visits and observations
  • Regional case studies or archives
Example
"Interview 2-3 professionals in your local area about [topic] and analyze their responses using course frameworks."

Personal Experience

Require integration of personal narrative and metacognition.
  • Personal narratives connected to concepts
  • Reflective analysis of lived experiences
  • Metacognitive reflection on learning
  • Application to personal goals
Example
"Analyze a conflict you personally experienced using three theoretical frameworks from class, explaining how each would interpret the situation differently."

Beyond What AI Can Do

Incorporate physical, oral, or real-time elements.
  • Oral presentations or defenses
  • Live demonstrations or performances
  • Hands-on physical tasks
  • Real-time in-class problem solving
Example
"Present your analysis in a 10-minute oral defense where you'll respond to questions about your methodology and conclusions."
