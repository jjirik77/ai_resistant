Humanities AI-Resistant Assignment Design

Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Follow the journey from a vulnerable prompt to a secure, authentic assessment.

The "Essay"

Status: Highly Vulnerable
ENG 101: Introduction to Literature Fall 2024
Midterm Essay: Thematic Analysis

Instructions: Write a standard 5-page academic essay analyzing the intersection of madness and revenge in Shakespeare's Hamlet.

Your essay should utilize at least three scholarly sources, demonstrate close reading of the text, and follow proper MLA formatting constraints. Focus on character motivation and plot development.

  1. Analyze themes in Shakespeare's Hamlet.
  2. Write a 5-page standard essay.
  3. Synthesize madness and revenge.
Vulnerability Report
  • Universal Training Data: AI has access to millions of essays on Hamlet. It knows the text better than the student.
  • Text-Only Output: The "5-page essay" is the native output format of Large Language Models.
  • Zero Hallucination Risk: The topic is settled and generic. AI will not make factual errors here, making detection nearly impossible.

The Humanities Shield

To transform the vulnerable assignment, we apply four key strategies that introduce human friction and context.

1. Contextualization

Anchor abstract concepts in specific reality. Connect "Hamlet" to a local event happening this week.

2. Multimodality

Shift the output. AI is text-dominant. Visual curation and spatial arrangement (timelines) break the model.

3. Personal Synthesis

Require the "I". AI cannot authentically write about how a text changed your specific perspective.

4. Metacognition

Assess the journey. Ask students to document why they made specific choices.

The "Project"

Status: AI Resistant
ENG 101: Introduction to Literature Fall 2024
Midterm Project: "Hamlet in the Neighborhood"

Instructions: Create an interactive digital timeline connecting the themes of madness and revenge in Hamlet to three specific local events occurring in our city this month.

  • Mapping: Place 3 plot points from the play alongside 3 real-world local news stories.
  • Reflection: Record a 2-minute video reflection explaining why you selected these specific local events.
  • Synthesis: Annotate your timeline with specific quotes connecting the text to the modern context.
  1. Connect specific themes to three local current events.
  2. Build a digital multimedia timeline (images + text).
  3. Include a reflective video explaining your interpretation.
Verification Successful

This assignment successfully applies the framework strategies:

  • Contextualization: Connecting to "Local Events" forces the student to use data the AI does not have access to (hyper-local news).
  • Multimodality: Changing the output to a "Timeline" breaks the text-generation pattern of the LLM.
  • Metacognition: The reflective video proves the student is the author of the thoughts.
