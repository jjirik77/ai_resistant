Social Science AI-Resistant Assignment Design

Move beyond "detecting" AI to "designing" around it. Follow the journey from a vulnerable prompt to a secure, authentic assessment.

The "Paper"

Status: Highly Vulnerable
SOC 201: Urban Sociology Spring 2025
Final Research Paper: Gentrification

Instructions: Write a 5-page research paper analyzing the impact of gentrification on urban communities in the United States.

Discuss economic shifts, displacement, and community identity. Use at least 5 scholarly sources to support your arguments.

  1. Analyze impact of gentrification.
  2. Write a 5-page standard research paper.
  3. Synthesize general economic shifts.
Vulnerability Report
  • Universal Training Data: "Gentrification" is one of the most common topics in sociology. AI has ingested thousands of papers on this exact prompt.
  • Text-Only Output: The 5-page format matches the AI's native text-generation capabilities perfectly.
  • Generic Synthesis: Without a specific location requirement, the AI can hallucinate a "perfectly average" essay that is technically correct but lacks insight.

The Social Science Shield

In Social Sciences, we defend against AI by requiring students to become the primary source of new data through field research and local application.

1. Authentic Data

Data must be local and first-hand. If the answer is on Google, ChatGPT has it. Force students to generate new data via interviews or observation.

2. Process Focus

Assess the research journey. Require interview transcripts, field notes, and proposals. AI is great at the product, bad at the messy process.

3. Applied Theory

Don't ask "What is Equity?" Ask "How does the Equity Framework apply to the parking ticket data you collected yesterday?"

4. Singular Context

Encourage unique combinations. A student analyzing this specific library with these 3 interviewees creates a dataset of one.

The "Project"

Status: AI Resistant
SOC 201: Urban Sociology Spring 2025
Local Policy Impact Analysis

Instructions: Analyze the real-world impact of a recent municipal decision by conducting primary field research.

  • Selection: Choose a policy enacted by the city council or school board in the past 12 months.
  • Fieldwork: Interview 3-5 affected community members and attend one relevant government meeting.
  • Synthesis: Apply course theories (Stakeholder Analysis, Equity) to your specific findings.
  1. Interview 3-5 affected community members face-to-face.
  2. Select a hyper-local policy from the last 12 months.
  3. Attend one government meeting (proof required).
Verification Successful

This assignment successfully applies the framework strategies:

  • Authentic Data: Conducting interviews generates original data that no AI model has access to.
  • Singular Context: Focusing on a recent (last 12 months) hyper-local policy leverages the AI's "knowledge cutoff" and lack of local training data.
  • Process Focus: Requiring proof of attendance (meeting) adds physical friction that text generators cannot bypass.
