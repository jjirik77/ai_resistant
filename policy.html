Institutional AI Policies

A comprehensive guide for faculty to navigate academic integrity, security, and policy implementation in the age of AI.

Navigating Policy Landscapes

As you implement AI-resistant assignments, understanding your institution's AI policy landscape is critical. Institutions typically fall into one of three categories. Click cards to explore.

Prohibitive

Bans AI use outright. Focuses on detection and strict enforcement of academic integrity violations. Often leads to an "arms race" between detection software and generative models.

Permissive

Allows AI use with proper attribution. Treats AI as a tool similar to a calculator or spell-checker. Faculty must clearly define "proper attribution" to avoid confusion.

Pedagogical

Focuses on learning objectives over restrictions. Designs assessments where AI use is either integrated or ineffective. Encourages critical analysis of AI outputs.

Key Policy Components

What to Address

  • Define tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.)
  • Permitted vs. Prohibited contexts
  • Attribution requirements
  • Data privacy & FERPA

Implementation

  • Assessment design recommendations
  • Consequences for violations
  • Accessibility considerations
  • Faculty autonomy levels
